## What Went Well

1. **The "gap found during story creation" pattern caught real architecture gaps before they became blockers, five times in a row.** Story authors cross-referenced the epic text against the *actual merged code* (not just the epic's paraphrase) and found: 6.1's Flex Consumption plan requires an Event-Grid-sourced blob trigger (the default polling trigger silently never fires on this hosting plan); 6.2's pipeline had no `PlugId` anywhere despite every AC requiring one; 6.3's pipeline had no way to recover the original filename Meross's device-name extraction depends on; 6.4's `ReconciliationEngine` had no defined trigger point since nothing in the architecture produces an "all plugs done" signal; 6.5's validator required `EuLabelClass` when the actual UX decision made it optional. Every one of these would have been a mid-implementation blocker or a silently wrong shipped feature if taken at face value.
2. **`MerossParser`'s deliberate divergence from `EveHomeParser`'s exception-handling pattern.** Story 6.3 explicitly reasoned about *why* copying the sibling parser's blanket `catch (Exception) → UnreadableFileException` wrapper would silently break two pre-existing tests, and got the design right instead of pattern-matching on the nearest neighbor.
3. **Ralf's live manual walkthrough of Story 6.6 caught what automated review structurally could not** — a UX-level design flaw (the device-association dropdown let you pick a *Device* but not the *PowerPoint/Anschlussstelle* itself, breaking for any shared smart-plug strip) that no code review would surface without a human actually using the flow. The team redesigned the dropdown on the spot per his feedback.
4. **Epic 5's decimal-precision validation policy — flagged critical-path-before-Epic-6 — landed on 2026-07-05, before Story 6.0 even started.** Second epic in a row where a pre-epic commitment shipped as a dedicated piece of work ahead of the first story, not squeezed into it.
5. **Test discipline held for the whole epic** — 324 backend / 293 frontend tests, zero regressions reported in any of the seven stories' verification passes.

## Challenges & Patterns

1. **A correctly-predicted, explicitly-written risk sat unactioned for five stories and then broke in production.** Story 6.1's own code review (2026-07-06) named the exact defect — enums serializing as raw integers over `ObjectResult` responses — and said in writing: "will need fixing before Story 6.6's frontend consumes this field." It was logged in `deferred-work.md` and carried through Stories 6.2, 6.3, 6.4, 6.5, and 6.6's own reviews without being picked up, then broke the Import Progress Card live on Azure the day after 6.6 shipped, requiring a full forensic investigation to re-derive what was already written down. **Root cause (Ralf's read): `deferred-work.md` isn't checked at story-creation time** — the log accumulates correctly but nothing re-surfaces an item when the story it names as a trigger is actually being created.
2. **Epic 5's Action Item #1 (cascade-delete helper) landed half-done.** The shared helper (`LoadFlatCascadeChildrenAsync`) was extracted before Epic 6 started, with its own comment saying "extend this method when a new Flat-scoped child table is added" — but Story 6.1 never extended it for `ImportJobs`/`SmartPlugDailyData`/`SmartPlugIntervalData`. No production impact (real SQL Server FK cascade handles it), but it silently reintroduces the exact drift risk the Epic 5 retro flagged, and the InMemory test suite won't exercise cascade-delete correctness for these three tables.
3. **Review volume was high but consistently converged in one round per story** (except 6.6, which needed a second round driven by Ralf's live walkthrough) — not a concern on its own, but a reminder that "gap found during story creation" stories (5 of 7 this epic) correlate with higher review-finding counts, since the AC set itself grew mid-story.

## Technical Debt (logged, not blocking — see `deferred-work.md` for full detail)

- Broad `catch (DbUpdateException)` in both `EveHomeParser` and `MerossParser` mischaracterizes any DB failure as benign "concurrent import, skip" — cross-story systemic pattern, best fixed holistically.
- `ReconciliationEngine.BuildMainMeterDailySeries` can double-count same-calendar-day meter readings — mirrors a known pre-existing `KpiCalculator` gap; the two are intentionally kept in sync rather than fixed independently.
- DST fall-back hour collision in `EveHomeParser`'s timestamp-based dedup — accepted, once-a-year single-hour data loss.
- No focus management / `aria-live` region on Story 6.0's delete-confirm rows in the Flat Structure editor.
- No optimistic concurrency control on `Flat`/`Room`/`PowerPoint`/`Device`/`Tariff`/`MeterReading` — recurring, architecture-wide, not Epic-6-specific (only the three Story 6.1 tables have `RowVersion`, deliberately scoped).
