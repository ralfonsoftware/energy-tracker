## What Went Well

1. **The Epic 4 retro's hard blocker was honored, not waved through.** Action Item #1 (`EffectiveDate`/`ContractStartDate` redesign) explicitly gated Epic 5's start; it shipped via Story 4.4 before Story 5.1 began, and Story 5.1's own Dev Notes cite this directly.
2. **Both "critical path" prep items from the Epic 4 retro landed as dedicated commits before any Epic 5 story began** — `localDate.ts` extraction (`38ce9ca`) and the `CompleteOnboardingFunction`/`PatchFlatFunction` test backfill (`4ee466a`), both 2026-07-03. The Epic 3→4 soft-commitment slippage did not repeat.
3. **The `.LoadAsync()`-before-cascade-delete pattern scaled cleanly across three stories.** Story 5.1 established it for one level and explicitly wrote down the obligation for future Flat-child tables; Story 5.3 followed that instruction for a three-level hierarchy without rediscovering the InMemory-cascade gotcha from scratch.
4. **A real, forward-looking bug was caught before it could bite Epic 6.** Story 5.4's review found the structure editor's save-mapping hardcoded `consumptionApproach: 'None'` on every device on every save — which would have silently wiped any device's real consumption profile the moment Epic 6 Story 6.5 populates it. Fixed now, before Epic 6.5 exists to expose it.
5. **Epic 4's retro action items #6/#7 (UX polish) converted directly into a clean, scoped Story 5.5** — the retro-to-epic pipeline worked as designed.
6. **Debt triage discipline held up under scrutiny.** Cross-referencing the full `deferred-work.md`: items deferred in earlier epics get genuinely revisited when they become load-bearing (the tariff tie-break, the KPI cost-gap floating-point bug) rather than silently forgotten.

## Challenges & Patterns

1. **New territory generates more validation gaps than familiar territory.** Story 5.3 (first nested request payload, first entity enums in this codebase) drew 6 review patches, several around validation gaps (`IsInEnum()`, cross-field requirements, missing `MaxLength`) that mature slices no longer need reminding about. Not a regression — an expected cost of breaking new ground. Worth budgeting a heavier review pass whenever a story is "the first X."
2. **A manual verification step was skipped in the story record without the record being corrected.** Story 5.5's Task 6 (real device/Safari check) was marked as not performed in the story file; it was, in fact, performed by Ralf, just never recorded. Low-stakes here since it was confirmed during this retro, but a reminder to close the loop on manual steps at the time they happen, not weeks later.
3. **A genuine cross-epic risk was identified: cascade-delete duplication is about to triple.** The `.LoadAsync()` cascade sequence is already duplicated verbatim between `DeleteFlatFunction` and `UpdateFlatStructureFunction` (flagged in Story 5.3's own review). Epic 6 Story 6.1 adds three more Flat-child tables (`ImportJobs`, `SmartPlugDailyData`, `SmartPlugIntervalData`), which would create a third copy — real drift risk if not addressed before Epic 6 starts.
4. **Decimal-precision validation policy has been deferred since Story 4.1** ("systemic, needs a policy decision") and is still unresolved. Epic 6/7/8 add a wave of new decimal fields (`EuAnnualKwh`, `SelfMeasuredKwh`, budget/insight thresholds) — cheaper to decide the policy now than retrofit it across many fields later.
