## What Went Well

1. **Epic-text staleness was caught twice, not missed once.** Story 8.3 found the epic's own stated root cause ("`popover.tsx` has a stacking bug") was stale — the real, still-reproducible clipping bug was in `LocaleSettings.tsx`, a different, unaudited component `popover.tsx`'s prior fix pass never touched. Story 8.4 explicitly cited this as a lesson in its own Dev Notes ("verify current file state yourself... treat the epic's framing as a hypothesis, not ground truth") before starting its own design gate. This is knowledge transfer working as intended, story to story.
2. **The UX design-gate pattern (Story 8.4) worked cleanly.** The epic explicitly withheld the grid column-count spec and required a Sally-proposed, Ralf-approved design before any code. Sally proposed 1→2→3 columns with an explicit `SmartStripCard` full-span decision; approved as proposed, no rework. Ralf called this out unprompted as the standout of the epic — "went very smooth." The same gate-then-implement pattern was reused mid-story for an unplanned addendum (sub-device grid inside `SmartStripCard`) after Ralf asked for it live, without needing a whole new story.
3. **Manual live verification happened this epic, closing the gap Epic 7's retro flagged.** Ralf tested Story 8.2 on real iPhone Safari and found two genuine defects automated tests structurally could not catch (dynamic toolbar behavior, viewport clipping) — both fixed same day. This directly satisfies Epic 7 retro Action Item #4.
4. **A premature "done" claim was caught by process, not by luck.** Story 8.2's Task 5 checkbox for the AC6 Safari check was marked `[x]` before the check had actually happened. Code review caught the mismatch, corrected the checkbox, and blocked the story from reaching `done` status until the real verification landed a day later. The safety net worked exactly as designed.
5. **Real design decisions were surfaced to Ralf explicitly, not silently resolved by the dev agent.** Story 8.1's payload-building approach (draftRooms vs. lastSaved) and room-correlation strategy (index vs. key), and Story 8.4's grid-gap tradeoff (sparse auto-placement vs. `dense` reordering), were all flagged as `[Review][Decision]` items and resolved by Ralf directly rather than assumed.

## Challenges & Patterns

1. **Save-affordance consistency is only partially solved — three different visual patterns still exist.** Ralf's own usage after deployment: the room-list view has per-room Save buttons *and* a separate top-right page-level Speichern button coexisting; the power-points view has a differently-styled save action that "may not be visible until you scroll" with a few power points; the device-edit screen's Cancel/Save row still sometimes needs a slight scroll on Safari. Each of Stories 8.1/8.2 individually passed its own ACs and tests, but no story ever looked at all three save contexts side-by-side as one design language — the epic's original ambition ("save actions are consistent... across the whole app") is not yet fully realized. Sally's read: this needs a dedicated unified design pass, not another point fix.
2. **Whether the power-points-view scrolling complaint is a `StickyActionBar` regression or the room-list's per-room Save button behaving as designed is not yet disambiguated.** Story 8.2's `StickyActionBar` was built specifically to guarantee visibility regardless of content length; if it's failing to do that, it's a real regression worth root-causing before any redesign work starts.
3. **`deferred-work.md` has grown across eight epics with no cleanup pass ever performed.** It now mixes genuinely stale/resolved entries, low-value noise, and real unaddressed product debt (e.g., the Smart Power Strip "unconfigured remainder" formula, the two Epic-7-carried follow-up stories on unmeasured-device treatment and PowerPoint deep-linking) with no prioritization or triage. Ralf flagged this as something he wants actively shortened and prioritized, not just continuously appended to.
4. **The "second-look review pass on non-negotiable-rule patches" trial (carried since the Epic 4 retro) went a fourth consecutive epic with no decision — resolved this retro, see Action Items.** Winston's read: the trial targeted a specific risk class (silently overriding a documented non-negotiable invariant) and Epic 8 had no case matching that risk, consistent with the pattern across all four epics it sat dormant.
5. **`StickyActionBar` positioning cannot be verified by automated tests** — jsdom does not model real CSS/compositing behavior, so its correctness depends entirely on manual checks. Story 8.4's own manual viewport pass (375/800/1280px) was not completed in-session, leaving a real (if likely low-risk) verification gap at epic close.
