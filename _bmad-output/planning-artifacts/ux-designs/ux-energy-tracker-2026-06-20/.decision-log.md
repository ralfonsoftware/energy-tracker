# Decision Log — energy-tracker UX

## Session: 2026-06-20

### D-1: Form factor
**Responsive web, mobile-first.** Phone and tablet are the primary surfaces; desktop supported as responsive layout. Not a native app (v1/v2 scope).

### D-2: Cold open
**Dashboard first.** User lands on KPI dashboard with a large, prominent "Enter Reading" CTA. Entry doesn't require a full-screen context switch — dashboard is the home.

### D-3: Design reference
**Apple Weather app energy + liquid glass aesthetic.** Atmospheric feel, bold numerics dominant, glass panels with backdrop-filter blur, depth through layering. Ambient background adapts to consumption state.

### D-4: Theme
**Light and dark.** Both modes required. Dark may be the more natural mode for evening basement reads.

### D-5: Component library
**shadcn/ui** on a responsive web stack. DESIGN.md specifies brand-layer delta only; shadcn defaults inherited for all unlisted tokens.

### D-7: Gradient anchor — kWh budget
Gradient midpoint = daily kWh budget (Annual kWh Baseline ÷ 365). −50% of budget clips to cool edge; +50% clips to warm edge. ±10% of midpoint = acceptable zone, no visual alarm. Midpoint marker: a 1px semi-transparent hairline across the background (no label). Budget delta in the daily KPI tile: kWh delta (e.g., "↓ 0.8 kWh under budget"), not € delta. kWh chosen as anchor over € because kWh usage is stable across tariff changes; € figures shift with every tariff update (up to every 12 months).

### D-8: KPI hierarchy — kWh primary, € secondary
kWh is the primary orientation metric. € cost is shown on the dashboard but secondary in the visual hierarchy. Gradient encodes kWh position vs budget, not € position.

### D-9: KPI tile layout — paired
Each KPI tile pairs kWh headline with its € equivalent as a subline in the same card. Daily tile additionally shows the budget delta as tertiary text: "↓ 0.8 kWh under budget". Compact, correlated, no separate cost section.

### D-20: Enter Reading sheet — two fields
Two inputs: (1) numeric kWh value, required, large, auto-focused with numeric keyboard on open; (2) date + time, pre-filled with current timestamp, editable for retroactive entries. Normal flow: enter one number, tap Save, done. Retroactive: change the prefilled date/time before saving. No separate "retroactive mode" toggle — the prefilled field makes it transparent.
Inline validation: if entered value is below the last recorded reading, show a warning "Lower than your last reading (42,187 kWh) — is this correct?" with the option to proceed (handles meter replacement or corrections).
Hint inside the sheet: "Date and time will be saved with your reading."

### D-43: Smart Power Strip — physical model in Flat Structure
Two hardware types exist: Smart Plug (single outlet, one device) and Smart Power Strip (N outlets, N devices). The Flat Structure model extends to accommodate this:
  Power Point (wall socket) → Smart Plug → one Device (measured, direct)
  Power Point (wall socket) → Smart Power Strip → multiple Strip Outlets → each has a Device (estimated)
The strip itself is what gets associated to the import file. The strip's measured total is the authoritative kWh figure; per-device values within the strip are estimates.

### D-44: Smart Power Strip — decomposition split behavior
Strip measured total is divided proportionally across connected devices:
  Device share = (device_estimated_kWh / sum_of_all_configured_estimates) × strip_measured_total
Unconfigured devices (no EU label, no self-measured value): receive an equal share of the remaining proportion (strip_total minus configured_devices_share ÷ unconfigured_device_count). Displayed with lighter/dimmed color and a "Configure device profile for a more accurate split" hint.
Decomposition card layout: large strip card (measured badge) with an inner sub-section listing per-device rows — configured devices at normal opacity, unconfigured at reduced opacity with the configure hint. Card header shows strip total (measured); sub-rows show estimated splits.

### D-41: Device Registry — EU energy label fields
Annual kWh figure is the required field (drives decomposition calculation). Energy class (A/B/C/A+/A++/A+++ etc.) is optional — stored for potential future insight use (e.g. replacement candidate detection). Both fields appear on the EU label form; only kWh is marked required.

### D-42: Device Registry — self-measured input
A toggle selects the measurement period: Daily or Weekly. A single kWh input field below the toggle accepts the value. "Daily" pre-selected as default. Simple, no ambiguity.

### D-36: Smart Plug Import — surface and trigger
Persistent import icon in the Decomposition tab header (always accessible). Decomposition empty/unavailable state shows a hint + direct import button triggering the same import surface. Import surface flow: (1) multi-file selection (file picker; drag-and-drop on platforms/form factors that support it); (2) list of selected files appears below the upload zone — each file shows filename, auto-detected type (Eve Home / Meross), and a device association dropdown; if the filename contains the device name (case-insensitive), the device is auto-pre-selected; (3) "Upload Files" button initiates upload when all files have a device association. Multiple files can be uploaded in one batch.

### D-37: Smart Plug Import — processing feedback
After upload is initiated: a persistent progress card appears on the Decomposition tab and remains visible until background processing completes. User is not blocked — they can continue using the app. Consistent with the insight discovery progress indicator pattern (FR-39).

### D-38: Multi-flat switcher
Simple tap on the active flat name in the header reveals a dropdown with all flats + "Add flat" option. No separate switcher row needed. Works across all surfaces.

### D-39: Device energy approach — choice step
When adding a device, a choice step presents two options: EU energy label (class + annual kWh) or self-measured (daily or weekly kWh). User picks one, then fills in its specific fields. The other approach's fields are not shown. Both result in an "estimated" badge in the Decomposition view.

### D-40: File picker and drag-and-drop
Mobile: file picker only. Desktop/tablet (where platform supports it): both file picker and drag-and-drop on the upload zone.

### D-32: Decomposition — card layout, variable size
Room breakdown uses cards grouped by room (Apple Home app style). Device cards within each room vary in size by data richness: smart plug devices get a larger card with trend/usage detail; EU label or self-measured devices get a compact card showing only the estimated value. The visual hierarchy reflects data quality: rich data earns space, estimates stay compact.

### D-33: Decomposition — period selector
Dropdown at top of screen. Options: This week · This month · Last month · This year · Custom. Default: This month. "Last month" is a first-class option for early-in-month access to the most complete recent period.

### D-34: Decomposition — Residual placement
Residual summary card at the TOP of the decomposition view, above all room cards. Always visible without scrolling; never suppressed. Shows unattributed kWh and % of total.

### D-35: Flat Structure — dual entry points
Flat Structure is reachable from both: (1) Settings → Flat → Flat Structure quick link, and (2) the Decomposition tab empty/unavailable state ("Set up your flat" prompt). Both lead to the same Settings sub-screen.

### D-30: Reading history access — chart header icon
Reading history is accessed via a small clock/list icon in the trend chart card header, present on both the dashboard sparkline and the 30-day Insights chart. Tapping the icon opens a bottom sheet with the chronological reading list (date/time + kWh value + "corrected" note if previously edited). Tapping any entry opens the edit form pre-populated with its values (edit-with-log per D-16). Placement rationale: the trend bars are visual representations of readings — history access belongs in the same card.

### D-31: Spike detection — amber bar only, no separate alert
A spike is encoded visually as a distinctly styled amber bar in the trend chart. Full spike details (threshold, rolling average context) live in the Insights tab. No separate banner, badge, or notification is generated. The PRD's FR-17 "visible alert in the UI" is satisfied by the amber bar. PRD updated accordingly.

### D-28: Voice and tone confirmed
Precise, calm instrument register. Factual, never coaching or encouraging. Key patterns:
"↓ 0.8 kWh under budget" / "↑ 9.3 kWh over budget" / "— at daily budget" / "Couldn't save — try again." / "Locked — contract active until Dec 2026" / `Type "Wohnung 3B" to delete` / "No smart plug data for this period."
No exclamation marks, no streak language, no motivational copy anywhere in the app.

### D-29: Locale detection and override
Default locale is auto-detected from the browser's Accept-Language header. A small locale dropdown (e.g. `EN ▾`) sits in the top-right corner of the onboarding intro screen, allowing override before any data entry begins. Locale persists in browser-local storage after selection. The dropdown is present throughout onboarding and in Settings → Language & Region for subsequent changes.

### D-23: Onboarding — intro screen included
A brief intro screen precedes data entry. Contains: app name, one-line value prop ("Know what your energy costs, every day."), and a note that tariff information will be needed for setup — so the user arrives at the data steps knowing why. Single "Get Started" CTA.

### D-24: Onboarding — two data steps (not three)
Intro → Step 1: Name your flat → Step 2: "Your energy contract" (kWh baseline + tariff + derived euro budget). Steps 2 and 3 from the original plan are merged because tariff data enables auto-derivation of the euro budget. Step 2 collects: annual kWh baseline (presets or custom), tariff details (base fee, price/kWh, optional provider/contract info), and annual euro budget (auto-calculated from baseline × price/kWh + base fee × 12, prefilled, editable). Calculation shown transparently below the derived field.

### D-25: Insights tab — scrollable page, responsive layout
Trend chart at top, Generated Insights cards below — one continuous scroll, no segmented toggle. On tablet/desktop: chart and insights in a two-column layout (chart left or full-width top, insights cards in a responsive grid). Default chart period: 30 days. Provides broader information space than the dashboard sparkline and avoids redundancy with it.

### D-26: Insights tab — 30-day default trend period
30 days chosen over 7 to provide broader context; insights (standby offenders, budget pressure) require longer-period visibility and shouldn't repeat the dashboard sparkline.

### D-27: Settings — tariff nested under Flat, with quick links
Tariff management lives under Flat in Settings, not as a top-level Settings row. Settings screen shows a Flat card per flat (most users have 1–2) with inline quick links (Tariff · kWh Baseline · Flat Structure). Tapping the flat card opens the full flat configuration. App settings (Locale) and Account (sign out, flat deletion) are separate sections below.

### D-22: Post-submit confirmation — three signals, no success toast
On successful save: (1) sheet closes, (2) KPI tiles pulse with count animation as values update, (3) "Last read:" timestamp in the dashboard header updates to the new reading's date/time. Three ambient signals confirm the loop closed — no explicit success toast needed.
On save failure: sheet stays open (user's typed value is preserved), an error toast appears inside the sheet near the Save button: "Couldn't save — try again." with a Retry action. The user can retry without re-entering the value.

### D-21: Post-submit feedback — dashboard beat (B)
On save, the sheet closes and the KPI tiles pulse with a subtle count animation as they update to reflect the new reading. The changing numbers are the confirmation — no toast, no separate success state. The beat is the reward for completing the loop.

### D-19: Information Architecture — finalized
Four bottom tabs: Dashboard · Insights · Decomposition · Settings.
Trends and AI-generated insights share the Insights tab — trends are usage-pattern insights; standby offenders / replacement candidates / budget alerts are behavioral insights. Same mental category, one surface.
Decomposition is Release 2; tab present but locked in R1.
Enter Reading: bottom sheet triggered from Dashboard CTA, not a tab.
Onboarding gates first use (one-time, before tabs load).
Settings sub-screens: Flat config · Tariff · Flat Structure (R2) · Locale · Account.
Insights tab structure (R2 sections): Usage Trends (chart + spikes) · Generated Insights (standby offenders, replacement candidates, budget pressure, invoice deviation).

### D-18: Q-6 resolved — Flat deletion confirmation (B — type to confirm)
User must type the flat name to confirm permanent deletion. Matches Apple account deletion and GitHub repo deletion patterns. Friction is honest: it matches the irreversibility of destroying all readings, tariff history, and smart plug data for the flat. A simple confirmation dialog is too easy to tap through accidentally on mobile.

### D-17: Q-4 resolved — Budget settings placement (C)
Annual euro budget (planned annual spend for FR-37 alerts) is collected during onboarding alongside the Annual kWh Baseline — both are "how much do I expect to use / spend" inputs and belong together. In Settings, the euro budget lives near the tariff configuration, not in a separate budget section, because tariff and budget are financially related (price per kWh × budget = spending expectation).

### D-15: Gradient midpoint marker — removed
The fixed horizontal hairline marking the gradient midpoint is cut entirely. The background color shift (cool → warm) is itself a complete, unambiguous signal; the KPI delta tile ("↓ 0.8 kWh under budget") provides explicit precision. A fixed marker adds no information — it doesn't move with consumption, so it just marks the screen midpoint, which means nothing. A moving marker would reintroduce the thermometer metaphor that was already rejected. No marker; cleaner design, zero loss of meaning.

### D-16: Q-5 resolved — Meter reading error correction (C — Edit with log)
Readings are editable after submission. Editing records a lightweight note ("original value was X") visible in the reading detail. Single user, no regulatory immutability requirement. The "days later" case benefits from seeing the original value when reconciling against an annual bill. Balance: simplicity of direct editing + minimal audit trail.

### D-13: Q-3 resolved — Tariff lock enforcement
When a tariff entry's contract period has started, price fields render inline as read-only/greyed out with a small lock icon and a line such as "Locked — contract period active until Dec 2026." No dialog, no tap-to-reveal — the state is immediately visible on entering the edit form. Non-price fields (provider name, contract dates) remain editable.

### D-14: Tablet Enter Reading — icon-only button
On tablet, the compact header button uses a lightning bolt icon (Lucide `Zap`) instead of a text label. Prominent enough to find, small enough not to dominate the header. Consistent with Apple Home app-style icon actions in the navigation header.

### D-10: Gradient midpoint color — warm neutral
"At budget" (100% of daily kWh baseline) renders as a warm grey-charcoal neutral, not a cool slate. Ensures the midpoint doesn't read as "over budget." Cool indigo-teal = under; warm amber-orange = over; warm neutral = exactly at budget.

### D-11: Enter Reading button — label only
The button reads "Enter Reading" with no secondary hint text. The "timestamp auto-recorded" information moves into the entry dialog itself. Removing it from the button eliminates visual noise on the most prominent interactive element.

### D-12: Tablet — Enter Reading in content header
On tablet, Enter Reading is a compact button in the top-right of the content header (Apple Home app pattern). The full main content area is freed for the dashboard; the trend chart uses full width. Rationale: tablet is primarily a review device; meter entry almost always happens on phone (standing at the basement meter).

### D-6: Critical first-beat
**Meter reading entry**: user types only the kWh number; timestamp is auto-stored on submission. The round-trip (open app → persisted reading + KPI updated) must be under 60 seconds. This is the irreducible core.
